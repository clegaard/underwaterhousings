Whitetip reef sharks (Triaenodon obesus) found living their best lives in their favorite cave inside the reef surrounding Gili Trawangan, Indonesia 🇮🇩

Each individual may use the same hiding spot for months or even years and are often found huddling together in their preferred hiding spots within the reef 🦈

## 16
Just a few millimetres long, this one would have been completely invisible against anything neutral. What gave it away was the substrate it chose, a vivid orange sponge that turned it into a tiny piece of living jewellery.💎

‼️The contrast did all the work ‼️

ℹ️ Unidentia sandramillenae nudibranch wasn’t formally described by science until 2017, and its holotype was collected right here in Indonesia, at Tulamben, Bali by Sandra Milllen malacologist who spent nearly five decades advancing nudibranch taxonomy.
☺️That fact alone makes finding one feel like a small privilege.

🧐 Look closely at that body and it’s hard to believe it’s real. The cerata, running down its back are translucent purple, almost glassy, catching the light differently depending on the angle. Like other aeolid nudibranchs, Unidentia stores stinging cells inside those cerata, recycled directly from its cnidarian prey through a process called kleptocnidy.
🎨 The colour you’re seeing is a warning or possibly camouflage against the hydroids and soft corals it feeds on… Either way, it works.

🤿 Slow your dive down. The smallest things are usually the ones worth stopping for.💙

🎥 Filmed by David Vazquez Farrus
➡️ Follow @uw.ocean.visuals
📍 Tulamben, Bali, Indonesia 🇮🇩

Young coral heads were growing back everywhere you looked.

These particular reefs are not pristine because they go undamaged altogether, but because, isolated as they are from stresses like sewage, pollution, and overwhelming tourism, they are free to rebuild and rebound with amazing resilience.

I’m rooting for them.

## 19
Found the literal holy grail of the macro world today! 🤯🐑

My friend spotted a single piece of algae while we were diving here in Hawaii, and when we looked closer… it was an absolute VILLAGE. We counted around 35 Shaun the Sheep nudibranchs (Costasiella kuroshimae) all hanging out on one leaf!

But the universe decided to flex even harder. The leaf was covered in about 20 spiral egg rings. If you watch the video, you can actually see one of these tiny legends actively laying eggs.

🔍 Pro tip for watching: Notice the contrast in the egg rings? The older ones are darker and translucent, but the fresh ring being laid right now is a stark, brilliant white. Nature is absolutely wild.

Drop a 🐑 if you’re as obsessed with macro life as we are!
